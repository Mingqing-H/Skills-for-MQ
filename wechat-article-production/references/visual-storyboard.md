# Visual Storyboard

## Image Count by Length

- 1500 Chinese characters or fewer: cover + 2-3 article images.
- 1500-3000 Chinese characters: cover + 4-5 article images.
- 3000-5000 Chinese characters: cover + 6-8 article images.
- 5000-7000 Chinese characters: cover + 8-10 article images.
- 7000+ Chinese characters: cover + 10-14 article images or recommend splitting into two articles.

Default rhythm: one visual break every 500-800 Chinese characters or every 4-7 short paragraphs.

## Required Mix

- Cover image or cover prompt.
- Opening real visual or strong scene image.
- At least one framework/overview graphic.
- At least one real proof image for every major case or section when available.
- At least one code-rendered data chart for analytical articles over 3000 Chinese characters when numeric claims are central.
- One closing or summary visual for long essays.

## Mix Guardrails

- Real downloaded/captured materials should be the first choice and should usually account for at least 40% of正文图片 in research-heavy articles, unless sources are unavailable or legally risky.
- Generated images should usually stay below one third of正文图片, except for purely illustrative essays.
- Source cards should be fallback or supplement visuals. Do not let source cards dominate the article.
- SVG may be used during design, but final Markdown should reference PNG/JPG/WebP.

## For 5-Industry Articles

- Cover: concrete visual metaphor for five industries, not generic AI abstraction.
- Overview: one industry map or matrix.
- Per industry: first try one real proof image, such as official page screenshot, product photo, news title screenshot, or interface.
- Data/structure: at least one code-rendered line chart, bar chart, histogram, timeline, or workflow diagram. Prefer data-backed charts over decorative SVG.
- Closing: one summary quote/card or future map.
